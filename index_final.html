01 — Predictor Data Stack
⚗️
Geochemistry
31 layers
24 elements + 7 ratios. Key gold indicators: As, Sb, W, Bi, Mn, Cu — the same suite confirmed at Isulu and Bushiangala.
🛰️
Remote Sensing
3 layers
Sentinel-2 alteration indices: Clay-AlOH, Ferrous, Iron Oxide — tracing hydrothermal systems invisible to the eye in tropical terrain.
⛰️
Terrain (DEM)
4 layers
10m SRTM: Slope, Curvature, Plan & Profile Curvature. Topographic shape encodes what lies beneath.
🪨
Lithology

21 rock types one-hot encoded. The models learn which units host gold — Nyanzian greenstones score highest.
📐
Structure + Soil
3 layers
Distance to mapped faults — the strongest single predictor for orogenic gold. Plus soil organic matter and pH.
02 — Preprocessing Pipeline in ArcGIS Pro & Notebooks
Step 01
CRS Alignment
All 63 rasters aligned to UTM Zone 36N. Snap raster locks every layer to the same pixel grid.
›
Step 02
Resolution
Resampled to 10m cells. Bilinear for continuous data, nearest-neighbour for rock types. Clipped to county boundary.
›
Step 03
NoData Masking
County boundary applied as a mask. No values leak outside Kakamega into model training.
›
Step 04
Point Extraction
All 63 predictor values sampled at 409 training points via ExtractMultiValuesToPoints.
›
Step 05
SMOTE Balancing
68 gold sites vs 341 background. SMOTE generates synthetic positives to balance training before fitting.
03 — Training Data & Spatial Validation

409 training points: 68 confirmed gold occurrences + 341 background. A 5km spatial block grid (133 blocks) kept training and test data geographically apart — preventing the leakage that standard k-fold cross-validation cannot avoid.
