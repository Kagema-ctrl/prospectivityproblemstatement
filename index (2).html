02 — Preprocessing Pipeline in ArcGIS Pro & Notebooks
Step 01
CRS Alignment
All 63 rasters reprojected to Arc 1960 / UTM Zone 36N (EPSG:21096). Snap raster set to dem10 to guarantee identical pixel grids across all layers.
›
Step 02
Resolution Standardisation
All rasters resampled to 10m cell size using bilinear interpolation (continuous) or nearest-neighbour (categorical lithology). Extent clipped to county boundary mask.
›
Step 03
NoData Masking
County boundary polygon converted to raster mask. Applied to all 63 layers — ensuring pixels outside Kakamega County return NoData, not zero values that corrupt model training.
›
Step 04
Point Extraction
ExtractMultiValuesToPoints: all 63 predictor raster values sampled at 409 training point locations. Output: TrainingPts feature class with 67 fields (63 predictors + OBJECTID, Shape, Label, TxtLabel).
›
Step 05
SMOTE Balancing
Class imbalance addressed via Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique. 68 gold occurrences vs 341 background — SMOTE generates synthetic positive samples to balance training distribution before model fitting.
03 — Training Data & Spatial Validation Design

Training points were drawn from 68 confirmed gold occurrence localities within the Liranda Corridor — including the Isulu and Bushiangala deposits operated by Shanta Gold Ltd — paired with 341 stratified background samples representing non-prospective ground. Spatial cross-validation used a 5km fishnet grid (133 blocks) to ensure training and validation data were always geographically separated — preventing data leakage that conventional k-fold CV cannot avoid in spatial prediction tasks.
